Introduction

One portfolio from Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)

One portfolio from MPT (Markowitz (1959)) is the Global Minimum Variance (GMV) portfolio, given by \[ \min_\mathbf{w}\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{\Sigma}\mathbf{w},\\\;\; \text{s.t.} \;\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{1}= 1. \] where the solution is \[ \mathbf{w}_{GMV}=\frac{\mathbf{\Sigma}^{-1} \mathbf{1}}{\mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{\Sigma}^{-1} \mathbf{1}}. \]

Development of wealth

Not knowing what to own & diversification

  • In practice we do not know \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\), estimate with \(\mathbf{S}\) from \(n\) obs. How accurate is that estimate?
  • We want \(p\) to be large, e.g diversify a lot.
    • \(\mathbf{S}\) has \(p(p+1)/2\) parameters \(\rightarrow\) curse of dimensionality!
    • We want to use \(\mathbf{S}^{-1}\) for GMV, not \(\mathbf{S}\). How accurate is \(\mathbf{S}^{-1}\) or \[ \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{GMV} = \frac{\mathbf{S}^{-1} \mathbf{1}}{\mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{S}^{-1} \mathbf{1}}? \]
  • Estimates are ‘‚Äôuncertain’‚Äô \(\rightarrow\) can we understand that uncertainty or construct ‘‚Äôbetter’‚Äô estimates?

Shrinkage in high-dimensional portfolios

These papers consider portfolios on the following form \[ \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{SH}=\psi \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{GMV} + (1-\psi)\mathbf{b} \] where \(\mathbf{b}\) is “some portfolio.”

  • Introduces bias to the portfolio but will lower its variance.
  • How do we determine \(\psi\)? \[\min_{\psi} \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{SH}^\top \mathbf{\Sigma}\widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{SH}\] note that \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\) is present again!

Paper IV - Is variance informative?

  • Comparison between three different strategies to estimate \(\psi\): Frahm and Memmel (2010), Bodnar, Parolya, and Schmid (2018) & sample GMV
  • Compared by the empirical out-of-sample variance \(\hat{V}_{n_1, n_2} = \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{n_1}^\top \mathbf{S}_{n_2} \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{n_1}\) and the empirical relative loss \[ \hat{L}_{n_1, n_2} = \left(1-\frac{p}{n_2}\right)\hat{V}_{n_1, n_2} \mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{S}^{-1}_{n_1} \mathbf{1}-1 \]

Paper IV - Results

  • The Variance is informative, though bad for some \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\).
  • Dominance results/Ordering in loss and variance of the three different portfolios \(n,p \rightarrow \infty\).
  • Empirically ordering holds \(n,p < \infty\), example \(n_1=n_2\)

Paper III - Dynamic Shrinkage estimation

  • We work with \(\widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{n_i}=\psi_{n_i} \widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{GMV;n_i} + (1-\psi_{n_i})\widehat{\mathbf{w}}_{n_{i-1}}\) (nontrivial extension of Bodnar, Parolya, and Schmid (2018))
  • Estimate \(\psi_{n_i}\) by \(\hat{\psi}_{n_i}\) at each transition, equal/optimal when \(n,p \rightarrow \infty\)
  • Datasets can be overlapping, so Period 2 can contain data from Period 1!

Paper III - Results

  • We compare a number of different strategies, most notably Ledoit and Wolf (2020) nonlinear covariance estimator
  • Simulations show that the method is robust and often provide the smallest relative loss.
  • Empirically, Ledoit and Wolf (2020) is better at taking ‘‚Äôlow-risk’‚Äô positions, our method provides better out-of-sample variance, mean, turnover.
  • Methods are in the DOSPortfolio package (on CRAN!).

Paper V - ‘‚ÄôDouble’‚Äô shrinkage

  • An extension to the linear shrinkage, includes ridge type regularization to \(\mathbf{S}\) \[ \widehat{\mathbf{w}} = \psi \frac{(\mathbf{S}+ \eta \mathbf{I})^{-1}\mathbf{1}}{\mathbf{1}^\top(\mathbf{S}+ \eta \mathbf{I})^{-1}\mathbf{1}} + (1-\psi) \mathbf{b} \]

  • \(c \in [0,\infty)\) or \(p>n\), previously \(c \in [0,1)\) or \(p<n\).

  • We cant determine \(\eta, \psi\) analytically \(\rightarrow\) estimator of \(\widehat{\mathbf{w}}^\top \mathbf{\Sigma}\widehat{\mathbf{w}}\), consistent when \(n,p \rightarrow \infty\).

  • Have to assume that \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\) have bounded spectral norm

Paper V - Results

  • We compare to a number of different strategies, Ledoit and Wolf (2020), Bodnar, Parolya, and Schmid (2018), Frahm and Memmel (2010) for different \(\mathbf{b}\)
  • Simulations show that ‘‚ÄôDouble’‚Äô is also robust and on par with the Ledoit and Wolf (2020).
  • Empirically, Double performs well, example \(p=431, n=250\)

Summary

This thesis provides some ways to better understand the investment process or to provide ‘‚Äôgood’‚Äô portfolios when \(n,p\rightarrow \infty\). We either

  • assume a lot to get a deeper understanding of the quantities of interest.
  • assume little, construct ‘‚Äôgood’‚Äô portfolios but we might understand less.
